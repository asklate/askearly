Let's begin with what the verses say, and then we'll break them down. In the Book of Mormon, God separated the righteous
people from the wicked. The righteous people were called Nephites, and the wicked people were called Lamanites. The
Lamanites were cursed with a dark skin, which was a sign of their wickedness. The Nephites were blessed with a white
skin, which was a sign of their righteousness.

> And he had caused the cursing to come upon them, yea, even a sore cursing, because of their iniquity. For behold,
> they had hardened their hearts against him, that they had become like unto a flint; wherefore, as they were white, and
> exceedingly fair and delightsome, that they might not be enticing unto my people the Lord God did cause a skin of
> blackness to come upon them.
>
> And thus saith the Lord God: I will cause that they shall be loathsome unto thy people, save they shall repent of
> their iniquities.
>
> And cursed shall be the seed of him that mixeth with their seed; for they shall be cursed even with the same
> cursing. And the Lord spake it, and it was done.

Now, let's break this down. The first verse says that the Lamanites were cursed because of their iniquity. They had
hardened their hearts against God, and they had become like flint. The second verse says that they became a loathsome
people, this is so the Nephies wouldn't mix with them. If they repent of their iniquities, they can be forgiven and
the curse of darkness would be lifted. The third verse says that if anyone mixes with the Lamanites, they will also be
cursed with the same curse. Basically telling the Nephites to stay away from the Lananties.

It sounds kinda sketchy, right? Why would God curse a group of people with a dark skin? And thwy would they be
described as loathsome? Like they're disgusting or something? They're people! I mean sure they defied God, whatever.
But they were still people. The verses reek of racism. It's like saying, "Hey, you know those people over there? They're
bad, and they look different from us. We should stay away from them." It's not a very nice message.

All because a group of people chose to go and do their own thing instead of following God's chosen prophet. Does this
mean that everyone in heaven is going to be "white and delightsome?" What about the people of different races? The
people who have a rich history and culture? Are they going to be turned into white people in heaven? That doesn't seem
right.

I wonder if the curse of darkness was meant to be taken literally, or if it was meant to be a metaphor for the spiritual
darkness that the Lamanites were in. Did the Nephites cast the Lamanites out because of their skin color? Did they
shame them because they were "more righteous?" The scriptures are written from one viewpoint, those of the righteous.
We don't really have a full picture of it all. If we had records of the Lamanites we might be able to get a better 
understanding of how they really were. But we don't, we have only the one side to look at.

They say history is written by the victor. In this case, it would appear to be the Nephites.
