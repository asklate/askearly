title: Article of Faith 9

date: Friday, May 1, 2026
---

> We believe all that God has revealed, all that He
> does now reveal, and we believe that He will yet
> reveal many great and important things pertaining to
> the Kingdom of God.

This article of faith tells us that God reveals things to his children. He still speaks to us to this day through
living prophets and apostles. As it states in Amos:

> Surely the Lord God will do nothing, but he revealeth his secret unto his servants the prophets.[^1]

The LDS Church believes in continuing revelation. That God speaks to his children through the Holy Ghost. The same
way Moses took the children of Israel through the Red Sea on dry ground.[^2] This means that God still cares about
us and speaks to us today.

There are few messages of "thus Saith The Lord" in these latter days. So it begs to question where the Lord or God
is leading His church. Revelation must come to the prophet of the church for changes to be made right? But are
they actual revelation or are they simply policy changes? Policy changes can be reversed, doctrine cannot.

But when the church indicates something is revelation, and then reverses it later saying it was just a policy change.
Well, where did that come from exactly? Just the mind of a man? Or was it God changing his mind?

[^1]: Amos 3:7
[^2]: Doctrine and Covenants 8:2-3
