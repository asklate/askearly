# The Galley

Major Jack O'Brien stood on a catwalk overlooking The Galley. He watched as shopkeepers were opening their shops for
the day. People had to work in order to survive, they had to work to eat and be able to sleep so they could get up
the next morning to work again. It was a vicious cycle. Resting his hands on the railing, he wondered how the working
class managed to get done what they needed to get done. Life on the station wasn't exactly that easy as far as he was
concerned. When it came down to money matters, life was especially hard for those not in the service.

O'Brien smiled as he saw a child help her parents open their fruit stand. She didn't have a care in the world, it
was such innocence that he had lost long ago. O'Brien wished he could have some of that innocence back, any of it
really. He missed such things in his life. Times seemed simpler when he was a child. Jack wondered when it had all
changed, possibly for the worst. He wondered what it was like to be a child growing up in these uncertain times.
Aliens could come knocking on their door at any given time, he had to be prepared for whatever was to come their way.

"There you are Dorf." Grilka said as she approached. "The computer said you would be somewhere near The Galley, but
didn't state exactly where. I had to do some searching. But I found you!" She kissed Jack on the cheek.

Jack smiled at Grilka. "Yes, you sure did find me." He said.

Grilka had a concerned look on her face. "When you didn't say anything this morning before you left, I got worried.
Is everything okay my darling?"

Jack nodded his head. "Yes love. Everything is fine. I just have a lot going on in my head at the moment is all. 
Nothing big. I'll be fine." He turned around and leaned on the railing, so he could face Grilka. Jack smiled at her.
"You're beautiful, you know that?"

Grilka grinned back at Jack. Her species didn't blush as it wasn't in their genetic makeup. But his words made her
feel warm inside. "Thank you." She said. "What are you doing way up here? Seems like it would be quite a fall if you,
well you know, fell."

Jack nodded his head and looked down. It would be quite a fall, she was right about that. Lucky for him there was a
railing there to prevent him from falling to his death. "Just clearing my mind." Jack said. "I like to take time now
and again to rethink what's important in life. These people I'm responsible for, all a quarter million of them. They
are my chief concern, you included. I have to protect you all at whatever the cost."

Grilka nodded in response. "I see, thinking about going to war with anyone anytime soon?" She teased.

Jack shook his head. "No, of course not. But if we were to go to war, I want this station to be ready for it." He 
paused. "I don't want anything to happen to you."

"I know." Grilka said. "I don't want anything happening to you either." She looked down at a shopkeeper and waved
at her. The woman waved back at Grilka. "Well, don't get too caught up in alien watching." She said. "You do have
a station to run." Grilka laughed.

Jack smiled. "Right." He said. "I'll see you tonight?"

Grilka nodded. "Yes, five o'clock sharp. Don't be late."

"I'll try not to." Jack said. He watched Grilka leave the catwalk as he turned his attention back to the people
below. They didn't seem to have a care in the world, and why should they? They were at peace for the time being.
Peace was good for business, Jack decided. Very good for business.

# C&C

Commander Kate Monson stood at her station, she was monitoring shipping lanes just outside Crimson Gamma's shield
perimeter. As long as ships stayed outside of that range, they could conduct any trade they wanted to without it
being searched by her team. Once they docked with the station, all bets were off and anything was fair game. She
smiled at the thought, what things would security find today?

An alert appeared on her console. Kate looked down and read it. It was a general security bulletin regarding a
renegade shipping supplier and for her team to be on the lookout for them. Suspicious activity had been recorded
on other stations across the sector. Kate sighed, just what she needed more drama. She pressed a button acknowledging
the alert.

"Commander, we're being hailed by the Command Ship Albatron." An ensign at communications said. "They want to
speak with whoever is in charge."

Kate sighed. "Put it through." She said.

A female alien with four arms appeared on the main viewing monitor. Her eyes were several centimeters apart, further
than normal human eyes, and were different colors. Both a shade of green. She smiled at the sight of the commander.
"Commander Monson I presume." The alien said. "It is my pleasure ot make your acquaintance. I am Captain Shekti of
the Command Ship Albatron. We have been patiently waiting in your shipping lane for over four hours waiting to dock.
Can you give me an estimate when that might be accomplished? I have some rather urgent business to attend to on the
station, and I can't afford to be late."

*Of course, you do.* Kate thought. *And of course you can't.* She concluded the thought. Kate smiled her best smile
she could muster that early in the morning. "I apologize captain, we are rather busy this morning. We will see to it
your ship is next in line."

Captain Shekti grinned from ear to ear. "Thank you commander. I appreciate it!" She was a little to excited for Kate's
taste that early. But to each their own.

"Ensign, direct that ship to Docking Port Sixty-Seven." Kate ordered. "Instruct security to perform a full search."

"Aye sir." The ensign said and went to work contacting security.

It was possible they wouldn't find anything and Kate was jumping the gun, but she wanted to make sure of it. She
couldn't afford to have any mishaps while she was on watch. The last thing the station needed were any surprises.

Down in Docking Port Sixty-Seven, security guards waited for a shuttle to dock. As the shuttle docked and the doors
opened, Security Chief Killpack and a handful of officers entered and met Captain Shekti halfway. "Pardon me ma'am." 
He said. "I have orders to search your ship. Routine inspection."

The captain didn't seem fazed by the search orders in the least. "Fine, do whatever you have to do. I must be going."
She said as she walked past the lieutenant.

"Roger that." Killpack said. "Men, let's get to work." He sat down at the shuttle's controls and piloted it towards
the alien spaceship.

# Back Lot Twenty-Six

Captain Shekti crawled out of the service crawlspace and into the back lot. Standing up, she waited for her contact
to arrive. The problem with waiting like this was she didn't know when they would arrive. Only the place of meeting
was given. She could be waiting for hours, maybe even days.

So she waited and waited and waited. Three days passed without anyone coming to visit. Shekti wondered if they were
ever going to show up. She was growing impatient as she waited.

A man exited the same service crawlspace Shekti had entered the back lot from. He approached the captain and smiled.
Walking towards the captain, he smiled. "Captain Shekti is it?" The man said.

"Yes." Shekti responded. "I received your message. It was ... cryptic."

The man nodded his head. "Yes, I had to make sure anyone who came across it wouldn't understand it. But just enough
to pique your interest. Which it appears that it did." He smiled. It was an evil smile, a smile not to be trusted
but Shekt didn't care, the man had a job for her and that was good enough.

"Why don't you tell me the details." She said.

"Right down to business, I like that." He responded. "I need you to transport an item." He continued. "It's not
really on the level, if you catch my drift. It will take some ... doing." That damn smile returned as he interlocked
his fingers.

Shekti hesitated. "Nothing dangerous I hope. I'm not about to ship weapons or explosives."

The man shook his head. "No, no, nothing like that. This is more delicate. You'll see when the shipment arrives." He
turned to go.

"Wait." Shekti said. "How will I know when it arrives?"

"I'll be in touch." He said behind his back as he exited the back lot.

further word. On the plus side, he had free reign of her ship to search for anything suspicious.
