# Madison's Quarters

Madison Park woke up as her alarm went off. "The time is oh six hundred hours." The computer said. Madison groaned. Why
did morning have to come so early? Getting out of bed, she got dressed for the day. Hopefully today would be a 
different day than the last month had given her. Looking out her bedroom window, Madison looked at Shuka below. She
envied the people of Shuka. They didn't have a station to upkeep. Their lives were probably easier than hers was.

"Computer, what does my schedule look like today?" Madison called out into the air.

The computer made a beeping noise before responding. "Mandatory staff meeting at oh eight hundred. dockworkers staff
meeting at oh nine hundred. Maintenance due on shuttles three, five, eleven, and fourteen. End of schedule."

Madison sighed. She nodded her head. "Right. Meetings and then work. Makes sense for a typical Monday." She was
tempted to cancel the meetings, but that wouldn't be possible. Important things needed to be discussed. Some things
she just couldn't get out of. If she left now, Madison could make it to the commissary and get some breakfast. The
synthesizer in her quarters worked just fine, but sometimes she just wanted to interact with other people. Madison
walked out the door of her quarters, she was going to do just that.

# Station Commissary

Madison sat down with her usual bowl of oatmeal. It was what she could afford every day and not go broke. The oatmeal
in the commissary tasted better than the synthesized stuff from her quarters. Madison wished she could have a farm
fresh egg, but the prices to transport those from Earth were ridiculously high. She didn't have the money for that
kind of food.

"This seat taken?" Lieutenant Jeff Killpack, the station's chief of security, asked.

Madison looked up with a smile. "It's all yours." She said gesturing to the seat across from her. Jeff sat down.

Jeff noticed the oatmeal on Madison's tray and compared it to his own tray, another bowl of oatmeal. "Great minds,
eh?" He commented.

Madison was in a daze. Her mind was anyplace but the food she was eating. It took her a moment to realize what Jeff
was referring to. Then it clicked. She smiled at Jeff. "Yeah, no kidding." Madison said acknowledging his statement.

Jeff scooped up some oatmeal onto his spoon and placed it in his mouth. He chewed on it for a moment. "Hey, is 
everything okay with you?" He asked. "You seem ... I don't know, distracted this morning."
