# Chapter One

## 1

Life had its moments. Greg wasn't a fan of most of it. But what could you do when half of your life was a secret.
The other half? Well that was more or less a lie. Greg didn't *want* to lie to people, but his condition made lying
inevitable. There was nothing he could do to stop it. Greg wasn't born in this time, that being the year 2026, he
was from a different era ... a distant in the future kind of era.

Greg had tumbled through a wormhole and ended up in the distant past. A place where Earth wasn't at war. Destruction
wasn't everywhere you looked. It was a nice change of pace. When Greg first landed on Earth, his confusion was very
apparent. As time moved on, he was able to grow accustomed to his new life. He had no hope of returning back to
his home and time.

## 2

Walking into his job at the hardware store, Greg smiled at the cashier, Linda. "Hey Linda, how are you this fine
morning?" He asked with his usual charm.

Linda looked up from her till and smiled back at Greg. "I'm doing great Greg, thanks for asking." She said continuing
to smile at him. Linda had a crush on Greg, but that wasn't something she was willing to tell him or anyone else
for that matter. "Hey, the boss wants to see you." She said brushing her red hair out of her face. Her blue eyes lit
up. Linda knew something, but she wasn't about to tell Greg.

"Thanks." Greg said. He walked further into the back of the store where his manager's office was. Upon reaching the
office, Greg saw that the door was open. He peeked inside. Hank, his boss, was seated at his desk going over numbers
or some other managerial duties. Greg knocked on the door.

Hank looked up from his paperwork. "Greg! Come in, come in." He said. "Have a seat."

Walking into the office, Greg sat down across from his boss. "Linda said you wanted to see me?"

Hank nodded his head. "Yes, yes I sure do." He grinned. "You've been doing an excellent job at work, and I wanted to
show you my appreciation. I'm giving you a pay raise, how does five more dollars sound per hour?"

Greg leaned forward and smiled. Five dollars? He really must be doing a good job. Such things were unheard of, 
especially in the hardware industry. "Thank you sir, I appreciate it very much."

"Just keep up the good work." Hank said. He reached across the desk and shook Greg's hand. "You'll see the pay increase
in your next paycheck."

Greg continued smiling. "Thank you again." He stood up and walked out of Hank's office. Today was a good day after
all.

Walking towards the front of the store, Greg ran across Linda again. "Hey." He said.

Linda looked up from her till and smiled. "Hey yourself."

Greg laughed. "I was wondering if you'd like to go out for dinner tonight. My treat."

Linda's smile grew. "You betcha!" She exclaimed. "I get off at eight." She paused. "But you know that. I mean we
*do* work together." She laughed.

Greg nodded. "Yeah, I know." He couldn't help but smiling. "There's this new sushi place that opened up down the
block, I was thinking we could go there."

"Sounds perfect." Linda agreed. "It's a date. Now, get to work." She teased.

Greg went to the back room to get his uniform on.

## 3

Eight o'clock came quickly. Soon Greg and Linda were locking the store's front door. Greg escorted Linda to his waiting
car. It wasn't as efficient as the car he drove in 4132, but it got him from point A to point B without any problems.
What more could a guy ask for? Exactly.

The Shushi and Cajun Seafood Restaurant was pretty much empty. The dinner rush was long gone. They were open for 
another hour. It was enough time for Greg and Linda to eat and enjoy themselves. Sitting down at a booth in the
corner, they ordered their food and drinks. The drinks came first, naturally, the food would come later.

Greg sipped on his Rum and Coke while Linda enjoyed her Long Island iced tea.

"What took you so long to ask me out?" Linda asked. "We've been working together for over two years now."

Greg smiled and thought for a moment. "Oh ya know, just looking for the right ... time I guess. I didn't want to
mess things up if I was reading signals wrong." He took another sip of his drink.

Linda giggled. "Oh the signals were there all along Greg. You just had to ask me out. I'm glad you finally did."

Their food came a few minutes later, they laughed and talked late into the evening as they ate. As the nine o'clock
hour rolled around, the waitress came to the table.

"We're closing soon." The waitress said. "Here's your check." She smiled as she waited for Greg to hand over a
credit card which he producded as if on demand. "I'll be right back." The waitress said as she walked away. Sure
enough, a few moments later she came back with Greg's credit card and a receipt for him to sign.

Greg looked to Linda, he wasn't ready to go for the night. But the restaurant was closing, so they had no choice.
Standing up from the table, Greg and Linda left the restaurant. Walking out to Greg's car, Linda looked at him.
"So, now what?" She asked.

Greg leaned over and kissed Linda. She returned the kiss.

Coming up for air, Linda smiled. "I can get used to this." She kissed him again.

The rest of the night was a blur ... for both of them.

# Chapter Two

## 1

The next morning, Linda woke up in a hotel room. Rolling onto her side, she saw that Greg wasn't in bed with her.
Sitting up, she wondered where he was. Then she heard the shower running. Linda smiled, she had an idea. Getting out
of bed, she walked into the bathroom. Sure enough, Greg was in the shower. Linda opened the shower door and stepped
inside.

"Hey there tiger." Linda said. "Mind some company?"

Greg indeed did not mind the company, he had been hoping for it. "Come on in, the water's warm." Greg replied.

Linda kissed Greg as she put her arms around him and pressed her body against his. You can imagine the rest.

## 2

Walking into work, Greg couldn't help but smile. He had just spent one of the most amazing nights and mornings
of his life. There was reason to celebrate and be happy. It had been several years since Greg had experienced any
kind of companionship let alone intimacy. He was happy it had happened and was glad he took the time to get to know
Linda instead of just jumping right into bed with her.

Greg walked passed the cashier counter like he had done for the past two years. "Hey Linda." He said. Greg stopped
midstep. Linda wasn't standing behind the counter. Greg was confused, Linda had left the hotel twenty minutes before
he did, she should have beaten him to work easily.

"Betty?" Greg asked the cashier. "Where's Linda?"

Betty shrugged her shoulders. "She called out sick. That's all I know."

Greg nodded. "I see, thanks." He walked into the back of the store where the locker room was. Pulling out his cell
phone, Greg dialed Linda's number. It went straight to voice mail. Was she avoiding him? Or wa she really sick like
she reported to Betty. He wanted to know. But it would have to wait, he had a shift to cover first. Changing into
his work vest, Greg exited the locker room and got to work.

He would try Linda's phone number another five times that day, all without success as they went to voice mail. He was
afraid that something was wrong. Greg made a note to go to her house as soon as he got off of work to check on Linda.

## 3

Eight o'clock rolled around. Greg clocked out and made a beeline for the door. Turning the lights out, Greg locked
the front doors to the store and walked quickly to his car, a 2025 Mazda. It felt like a practical choice when he
made the decision to buy a vehicle. Greg had ridden the bus the rest of the time he was in this time, well he had
grown tired of the bus system and wanted to drive himself. So he got a car.
