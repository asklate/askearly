# Introduction

Walking home one day from the train station, I came across a rather peculiar satchel. Opening the case, I found a
manuscript inside. I use the word "manuscript" loosely. It was a written document alright. But some parts didn't
make sense. I continued reading late into the night and far into the next day.

I present here the full document as is, with some annotations by the original writer and me to help clarify
what it is I believe is being read or interpreted. Either way, it is all here in black and white.

The author's name is John Landry. I only know this because the last page of the manuscript states his name with
a signature on it. John's signature, it was spelled out in what appeared to be a red substance. Blood maybe? I'm
not sure until I have it analyzed. I didn't want to wait for that though, it doesn't matter if it's blood or not.
What matters is this record needs to get out to the world. People need to read it. People need to understand it
to the best of their ability. It needs to be released.

# Chapter One

1.1 Time is everything. Ancient times, modern times, the future. It all comes down to one thing. The seconds on the
watch keep on ticking no matter who you are. I was born in the year of our Lord one thousand eight hundred and
six, on the fourteenth of October, in a small town called Essex in Hampshire County of Florida.[^1]

1.2 My mother, having died in childbirth, was not around to raise me. So it was up to my father to begin my 
education. My father did the best that he could considering our poor circumstances. We worked all day and well into
the night to keep the farm afloat. Farming was in my dad's blood, you couldn't keep him away from his farm work. Even
on the days he wasn't feeling good you would find him out working the fields.

1.3 After I graduated from high school, I planned on going to college. But the farm was too much for my dad to 
run alone, and he was too proud to hire help. So I decided to stick around and help out as long as he would 
have me. I know deep down inside my dad felt bad about me not attending college. But what else could I do?
So I stuck on, until the inevitable happened.

1.4 Dad died on my twentieth birthday, he was only fifty-two. I'll never forget how that day went. I had 
woken up early, dad usually made waffles on my birthday, and I was looking forward to them. I remember walking
downstairs to the kitchen. Dad was nowhere to be found. I checked the entire house, there was no sign of him.
I went outside to the barn and found my dad's lifeless body there. Some wood had fallen loose from the rafters
crushing him and killing him instantly. At least I *think* it was instantly. Either way, he had been dead for
hours. He probably got up earlier to do his chores so he would have time for my birthday. There's not a day that
goes by where I don't blame myself for his death.

1.5 I held onto the farm and continued my dad's legacy. After his death, the farm reminded me so much of the good
times we had together. Working together. Laughing together. All the things that makes life worth living. I never
decided to have a family of my own, for one specific purpose.

1.6 When I turned twenty-two, I decided to take a small vacation. I boarded a ship and sailed to England. Well I
*thought* I was headed to England. I have some ancestors there and I thought I would look around some cemetery
and see what I could find out about them. As luck would have it, the ship I was on got struck by lightning and sank
to the bottom of the ocean. All hands were lost. *Including* mine. That's right, I died when I was twenty-two. It's
no lie, I died on that ship in 1728.[^2]

1.7 The next thing I remember, I woke up on my family farm without any knowledge of how I got there. I sure didn't
come back by ship, I would have remembered that. It was like I was simply plucked out of the ocean and placed back
in my bed without a scratch on me. I couldn't fully explain it at the time, but I had to find out what had happened
to me. So, I tested a few theories.

1.8 I started out small at first. Testing my limits as it were. I cut myself, I still bled. That hurt, don't try
that as a go to if you ever find out you can't seem to die. I tried drowning myself in the bathtub. First I tried
to hold my breath. When I didn't pass out, I exhaled all the air from my lungs and inhaled water as quick as I
could. I died within seconds, and just like that? I woke up in my bed.

1.9 It would appear that I cannot die. Or, rather, if I *can* die I manage to survive the event and wake up with
memories of it all but without any harm to my body. The knife cut on my hand I made earlier that day? Fully
healed. I can't explain it. I only know that it happened, twice now.

# Chapter Two

2.1 I had some decisions to make. If I couldn't die, I couldn't age. At least that's what I deduced. I wouldn't find
out until years later, that I was right about that. I couldn't stay on the farm forever. People would grow suspicious
about a man who didn't seem to get older. The last thing I wanted to be was being accused of witchcraft. I wasn't
a warlock. We didn't need a repeat of the Salem Witch Trials.

2.2 A plan had to be made. I would have to move around every few years. It was the only option. This of course meant
I couldn't get close to anyone either. How do you explain to someone you love that you'll never grow old with them?
It's not an easy discussion to have with someone. They would never understand why. So relationships were out of the
question. A shame really.

2.3 I stayed just outside of town for another ten years before I sold the farm. I figured staying longer might
raise suspicion. When you're supposed to look thirty-two, but you still look twenty-two? Sure *some* people can
pull off a thing like that, but it wasn't common.

2.4 Selling the farm wasn't an easy decision. I thought about renting it, leasing it, something like that. But
that wasn't in the cards for me. When you've lived as long as I have, you tend to let things go easier and forget
about them. Soon the farm was just a distant memory.

2.5 Soon it was the 1800s. I found myself moving to Wast Virgina. It was a quiet town called Dunner. A strange
name if you ask me. But it was still a town that I was living in. I made a few, a very few number of, friends.
They weren't real friendships of course, no one knew my secret. I couldn't afford telling *anyone* that side of
me. At least, not at that time I couldn't.

2.6 I was a witness to the Louisiana Purchase. Not many people can say that! To be honest, it was quite boring.
Land sales are always quite boring when you're there in person. I'm sure someone will make a big deal out of it
later on in life, but at the time it was just a boring purchase that was made. The U.S. got the land it wanted
and that's all that mattered. I do think it cost too much, but what do I know? I'm a simple farmer. &c.[^3]
